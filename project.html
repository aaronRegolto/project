Understanding Color Vision

Types of Color Blindness

Color blindness is not actually blindness—it is a deficiency in the ability to distinguish certain colors. Each type has distinct characteristics and affects people in unique ways.

Red-Green Type

Deuteranopia

Absence of green-sensitive cones. Red, orange, yellow, and green appear similar, often as shades of yellow-brown. The most common form among males.

5% of males
Red-Green Type

Protanopia

Absence of red-sensitive cones. Red appears very dark, almost black. Green and orange can be confused with each other. Brightness of red is significantly reduced.

2.5% of males
Blue-Yellow Type

Tritanopia

Absence of blue-sensitive cones. Blue and green are confused; yellow and violet look similar. Rarer than red-green deficiencies and affects both sexes equally.

0.01% of population
Complete Type

Monochromacy

Total absence of color perception. The world appears only in shades of gray. Extremely rare—achromatopsia affects about 1 in 33,000 people worldwide.

0.003% of population
